Expand Your Zoological Knowledge Diddy Zoo offers an exciting range of educational courses tailored to engage teenagers aged 13-16 in wildlife conservation, animal care, and environmental stewardship. These programs are designed to nurture curiosity, hands-on learning, and a deeper understanding of the animal kingdom. Here’s a look at some of the courses available for this age group: 1. Junior Zookeeper Experience This immersive program gives participants the chance to step into the shoes of a zookeeper. Teenagers work alongside the zoo’s staff, learning about animal husbandry, habitat maintenance, and dietary planning. Activities include:

  • Preparing animal meals.
  • Cleaning and enriching enclosures.
  • Observing and recording animal behaviors.

    2. Wildlife Conservation Workshop
    Focused on global and local conservation issues, this workshop provides insights into endangered species and how zoos contribute to their protection. Teens participate in:
  • Research projects on endangered species.
  • Discussions on habitat conservation and climate impact.
  • Creating awareness campaigns for zoo visitors.
  • Duration: 1-2 weeks, with optional project extensions.

    3. Veterinary Basics for Teens
    Aimed at those interested in a veterinary career, this course introduces basic animal health care concepts. Students learn:
  • How to perform basic health checks on animals.
  • The role of vaccinations and common treatments.
  • Understanding animal anatomy and behavior.
  • Duration: A weekend intensive or a weekly evening course over a month.

    4. Eco-Warrior Program This course empowers teens to become advocates for sustainable living and environmental protection. Topics include:
  • Reducing waste and understanding recycling processes.
  • Building sustainable habitats for small animals.
  • The role of biodiversity in maintaining ecological balance.
  • Duration: Monthly sessions, with hands-on projects.

    5. Animal Behavior and Training
    Perfect for teens curious about how animals learn, this program covers the principles of animal training and psychology. Activities include:
  • Observing animal training sessions.
  • Practicing positive reinforcement techniques with smaller animals.
  • Designing enrichment activities for various species.
  • Duration: Bi-weekly classes over 6 weeks.

    6. Photography in the Wild
    Combining art and wildlife, this course teaches teens how to photograph animals effectively while respecting their habitats. Skills covered include:
  • Camera techniques for action shots.
  • Ethical wildlife photography.
  • Editing and presenting nature photos.
